Women of color, on average, experience a larger pay gap compared to white women. This means that not only do women, in general, earn less than men, but within the category of women, there are further disparities based on race and ethnicity. For instance, Black women and Hispanic women tend to earn less than white women for similar work. This intersectional aspect of the gender pay gap highlights the complex and interconnected nature of economic inequality. It is important to address both gender and racial disparities in order to achieve true equity in the workforce.
