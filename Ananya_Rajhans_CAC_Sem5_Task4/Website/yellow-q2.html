Early to Mid-20th Century:

  • In the early to mid-20th century, traditional gender roles prevailed, with women often relegated to domestic responsibilities. The wage gap was substantial, reflecting limited opportunities and societal expectations.
    Mid-20th Century to 1970s:
  • During the mid-20th century and into the 1970s, societal changes, including women's participation in the workforce during World War II, influenced attitudes. The gender wage gap narrowed somewhat, but disparities remained, particularly in higher-paying professions.
  1980s to 1990s:
  • The 1980s and 1990s saw increased awareness of gender equality issues. Legal advancements, including the Equal Pay Act, aimed to address wage disparities. However, the wage gap persisted, and occupational segregation remained a challenge.
    2000s to Present:
  • In the 21st century, the gender wage gap has seen incremental improvements. More women pursue higher education and enter traditionally male-dominated fields. Efforts to address pay transparency and advocate for equal pay have gained momentum.
