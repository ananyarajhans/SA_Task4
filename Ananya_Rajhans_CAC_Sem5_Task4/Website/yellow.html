The gender pay gap manifests as a "motherhood penalty" and a "fatherhood bonus," reflecting deeply ingrained societal biases. Studies indicate that mothers experience a decrease in earnings, losing 4 percent for each child, while fathers enjoy a more than 6 percent increase in earnings after having children. Even when factors like education and hours worked are considered, discrimination remains a significant factor. Mothers face cultural biases, seen as less committed and more distractible, resulting in harsher performance standards. In contrast, fathers are perceived as stable providers. The impact is most pronounced for low-income women, revealing a stark economic cost for families with fewer resources.
