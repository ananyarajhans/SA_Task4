Gender-driven industries are those characterized by a significant concentration of one gender in specific professions or sectors. Historically, societal norms and stereotypes have played a pivotal role in shaping these patterns. For instance, industries like nursing, teaching, and administrative roles have been predominantly associated with women, while sectors like engineering, technology, and construction have been male-dominated. Such gender-driven divisions contribute to the perpetuation of the gender pay gap, as certain professions traditionally held by women tend to have lower average salaries compared to male-dominated fields.
