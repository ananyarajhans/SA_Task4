Entry-Level Positions: Younger women may face a narrower pay gap when starting their careers, as they may be more likely to enter the workforce with similar qualifications and expectations as their male peers. Promotions and Advancement: As employees progress in their careers, the gender pay gap may widen. This can be due to factors like gender bias in promotions, lack of access to high-level positions, and the tendency for men to negotiate higher salaries. Ageism and Retirement: Both men and women may face age-related challenges in the workforce. However, older women may face additional barriers due to a combination of ageism and gender bias, which can lead to lower pay and reduced opportunities.
