In the early to mid-20th century, the gender wage gap was deeply entrenched in societies across the globe. There were relatively few women in the workforce, primarily due to cultural and societal norms that dictated that a woman's primary role was within the home. Those who did enter the workforce often faced barriers in terms of educational opportunities. Women were often directed towards more menial roles, such as assembly line workers or personal secretaries, and were largely absent from professional fields. The prevailing belief was that women were inherently less capable than men, both intellectually and physically, reinforcing stereotypes that limited their access to education and professional opportunities. These cultural norms perpetuated the idea that women should focus on raising children and maintaining households, rather than pursuing careers or positions of power.
